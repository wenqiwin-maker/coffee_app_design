Context

Choosing coffee often feels like a gamble.

Users want to explore flavors, but there's no clear, confident path to what truly fits.

Notes like "Rich & Bold" sound appealing, but users can't tell what they'll actually taste. One wrong bag = $18–30 wasted, and the journey is fragmented across 5–7 websites.

70% want personalized coffee recommendations
5-7 websites browsed before purchasing
200% subscription market growth in 5 years
$18-30 wasted on each wrong coffee purchase

Target Users

Everyday Home Brewers

Ages 25–40. Tech-comfortable coffee drinkers who brew regularly but aren't experts.

2-7 cups/week $15-30 per bag risk 5+ sources browsed 25M+ U.S. home brewers

Research

Research Insights

Synthesized from generative research and expert interviews.

Key Insights

  • Users want to explore different coffees while finding the ones that truly fit their taste.
  • Users mix online + offline discovery, but the journey is fragmented with no unified place to compare, track, or remember.
  • Users want low-risk experimentation. But trying new beans with full-size bags make it costly.
  • Users want to feel confident when choosing between similar options but lack clear, intuitive signals.

Findings

9/10 enjoy exploring diverse coffee and the main goal is to find coffee that truly fits their personal taste
9/10 switched between supermarket shelves → Amazon → YouTube/Reddit → cafés when choosing coffee
9/10 hesitated to try new beans because "one wrong bag = wasted money & wasted mornings"
7/10 felt unsure which coffee to choose among many similar options, and wanted clearer guidance on "which one fits me"

AI Expansion + Review Integration

User Pattern: Very High Business Value: High Tech Feasibility: Medium

Homepage Clarity + Quick Actions

User Pattern: High Business Value: Medium Tech Feasibility: High

Clear UX gains. "Quick AI Compare + Try Sampler" improves discoverability and engagement across segments.

Medium Priority Expanding depth & fixing friction
3

Recommendation Depth & Accessibility

User Pattern: High Business Value: Low-Medium Tech Feasibility: High

Most coffee e-commerce lacks personalized criteria. Quick to implement contrast cards with transparency improvements.

User Pattern: Medium Business Value: Low Tech Feasibility: High

Common UX fix, essential baseline. Low cost, ships together with Homepage improvements.

What I Learned

1. Start with people, not solutions

Coffee discovery is driven by desire to explore. HCD isn't only about fixing pain—it's about enabling aspiration.

2. Converge to define the right opportunity

From scattered behaviors → a clear, desire-driven opportunity.

3. Diverge to explore multiple paths

Comparing directions allowed me to choose the most meaningful, viable path.

4. Design is a continuous loop

User testing refined clarity, confidence, and guidance—core experience qualities.

5. Connect insight to execution

Translate human insights into coherent systems, not isolated features.

"Human-centered design is not about solving the loudest problem—it's about designing with empathy, clarity, and intention, from understanding people to shaping meaningful experiences."
